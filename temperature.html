Latitude vs. Temperature

Plot of latitude vs. temperature.

This chart shows the distribution of temperatures compared to latitude. In general we see an inverted parabola with lower temperatures at higher latitudes and higher temperatures a lower latitudes. The distribution is not centered at o degrees (the equator), because the data is taken from July. We must remember that seasonality is driven by tilt in the Earth's axis, not proximity to the sun (the Earth is closer to the sun during Southern hemisphere summer/northern hemisphere winter. During the summer solstice, the Earth is tilted at 23.5 degrees, meaning that at 23.5 degrees latitude North, solar radiation is striking the Earth perpendicularly. That is why we see the peak of the inverted parabola shape closer to 20 degrees latitude. The cluster of high temperatures at 30 degrees latitude are most likely from desert cities, as most desert regions are concentrated around 30 degrees latitude (north and south).
