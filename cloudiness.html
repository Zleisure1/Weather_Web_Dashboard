Latitude vs. Cloudiness

Plot of latitude vs. cloudiness.

This chart shows the distribution of cloudiness versus latitude. We see high cloudiness from 0 to 9 degrees latitude, the lowest cloudiness from 20-29 degrees latitude and the highest cloudiness at 70-79 degrees latitude. This is as expected as tropical rainforests are concentrated around the equator which is why the 0-9 degree latitude bin has the second highest average cloudiness. Deserts are concentrated around 30 degrees latitude and typically have less moisture, more sun and less cloudy days. Other low pressure regions which have more precipitaiton and therefore clouds occupy around 60 degrees latitude. The highest average cloudiness bin in 70-79 close to 60 latitude. The 70-79 bin has higher cloudiness than the 50-69 latitude bins. This may be because during northern hemisphere summer the Earth is tilted approximately 23.5 degrees making the 60 degree latitude bin closer to 80 degrees latitude. The high concentration of northern hemisphere cities to southern hemisphere cities may influence the average more than at lower latitudes.
